Community Involvement

When deciding to get involved in the redistricting process, whether you are doing it alone, or working with a group, involving your community can lead to rich insights about what should be included in your plan.

Creating a Group

If you are interested in creating a group

Other Types of Community Involvement

There are various types of ways to get your community involved in your efforts to participate in your community's redistricting process. Below is a list of types of ways to get your community involved.

  • Forums
  • Information Sessions
  • Email Listservs
  • Surveys
  • Blogs

Information to be Gained from Community Involvement

Getting your community involved in your redistricting plans can prove useful in multiple ways. Not only can getting community input help in goal creation and formation (which was mentioned in a previous module as one of the more important parts of creating your plan), but it can also prove helpful in some of the data gathering. We mention below several places where community involvement can be helpful in creating your plan.

  • Goal Formation and Clarification
  • Analysis of Current Proposed Redistricting Model
  • Data Gathering
    • Traffic Patterns
    • Population
    • Bus Routes
    • Neighborhood Boundaries
